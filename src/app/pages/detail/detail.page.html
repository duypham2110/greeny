Mô tả sản phẩm

Put simply, the public aspect of blockchains generally implies three interrelated things: 1) Anyone can use the blockchain, 2) anyone can serve as a validating node of the blockchain, and 3) anyone who becomes a node can, in turn, act as part of that blockchain's governance mechanism. In theory, this makes blockchains decentralized and democratic structures resistant to undue control or influence from any single party.By contrast, a distributed ledger generally doesn't enable any or most of these public features. It restricts who can use and access it (hence the “permissioned” terminology), and it also restricts who can operate as a node. And in many cases, governance decisions are left to a single centralized company or body. Compared to the ideal of a public, decentralized blockchain, it exists solely to serve the interests of a concentrated group of commercial players and interests.
